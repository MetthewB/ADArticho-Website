# ADArticho Website

## **The Laughter Lens**  
A data-driven exploration of thematic and stylistic shifts in humor across institutional **(The New Yorker)** and general **(Oxford)** contexts.

---

## **About the Website**

This website serves as the interactive companion to the **ADArticho** project, providing an overview of the datasets, methods, findings, and team behind the analysis. It is designed to guide users through the key aspects of the project and allow them to explore the results in an engaging and structured way.

---

## **Website Structure**

The website is divided into the following pages:

### 1. **Home (index.html)**  
   - Provides an overview of the project, including:
     - A brief introduction to the research.
     - The key research questions driving the analysis.
     - Highlights from the exploratory data analysis (EDA).
     - A summary of the humor classification task.
     - Links to explore more detailed pages.

### 2. **Datasets (datasets.html)**  
   - Describes the datasets used in the project:
     - **New Yorker Caption Contest (NYCC)**: Institutional dataset with captions and funniness scores.
     - **Oxford Humor in Context (OHIC)**: General dataset with image-text pairs and humor annotations.
     - A comparison of the two datasets.

### 3. **Models & Methods (models-methods.html)**  
   - Details the methodologies and models used in the analysis:
     - Data integration and preprocessing.
     - Thematic NLP and semantic modeling.
     - Comparative statistical and stylistic analysis.
     - Temporal and cultural trend analysis.

### 4. **Team (team.html)**  
   - Introduces the team behind the project:
     - Lists team members, their roles, and contributions.

### 5. **Sources (sources.html)**  
   - Provides references and resources:
     - Links to datasets, tools, and libraries used in the project.
     - External references for further reading.

---

## **How to Navigate the Website**

- Use the **navigation bar** at the top of the page to access different sections of the website:
  - **Home**: Overview of the project.
  - **Datasets**: Information about the datasets used.
  - **Models & Methods**: Details of the methodologies.
  - **Team**: Meet the team behind the project.
  - **Sources**: References and resources.

- Each page includes a **call-to-action** section with links to explore related content.
